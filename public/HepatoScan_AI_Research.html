Blockchain in Healthcare — Evidence Base

Blockchain Solves the Data Silo Problem

Research consensus: 3,800+ Scopus papers, 1,383 IEEE papers, and 537 PubMed papers on blockchain in healthcare. Current EHR systems have centralized architectures that create single points of failure, prevent interoperability, and make audit trails deletable. Blockchain solves all three — provably.

🚨 Hospital Data Silos
Patients who receive care at multiple hospitals have fragmented records. A patient scanned at Hospital A who collapses at Hospital B has zero imaging history available — Hospital B starts from scratch, risks wrong treatment.
Source: Frontiers Digital Health 2024 (doi:10.3389/fdgth.2024.1359858) · PMC Blockchain SLR 2024
🚨 No Cross-Hospital Consent
Current systems require patients to sign new consent forms at each hospital. Blockchain EHR research identifies this as a critical barrier: "when a registered patient travels, they may need to register again under the new controller smart contract." (Wiley HBE 2024)
Source: Wiley Human Behavior & Emerging Tech 2024 (doi:10.1155/hbe2/4734288)
🚨 No Immutable Audit Trail
PostgreSQL and traditional database logs are modifiable — privacy violations are undetectable. Research confirms that healthcare data breaches are rising, with "hacking/IT incidents" the most common type (HIPAA Journal 2023). No centralized system provides tamper-proof access logs.
Source: Frontiers Big Data 2023 (doi:10.3389/fdata.2023.1146023)
🚨 Emergency Data Access Delays
Under current systems, emergency cross-hospital data requests take 48–72 hours via fax or phone. Research on "break-glass" emergency access in blockchain EHR systems confirms this is a recognized unsolved problem in existing deployments.
Source: Wiley HBE 2024 · MediLinker Study, Frontiers Big Data 2023
🚨 Semantic Interoperability Gap
Different hospitals use incompatible data formats. HL7 FHIR standards exist but adoption is fragmented. Multiple blockchain EHR studies identify this as the #1 unsolved research challenge — our on-chain FHIR-compatible schema directly addresses it.
Source: Wiley HBE 2024 · Sustainability 2023 (doi:10.3390/su15086337)
🚨 NAFLD Non-Cirrhotic HCC Blind Spot
38.5% of NAFLD-HCC patients are non-cirrhotic, yet surveillance guidelines from AASLD, EASL, and APASL exclude them. These patients are diagnosed at advanced stages with larger tumor sizes and lower curative treatment rates. A cross-hospital AI system can flag at-risk patients proactively.
Source: Lancet Oncology 2022 (doi:10.1016/S1470-2045(22)00078-X) · Cancers 2024
